Lemon-Herb Grilled Chicken Thighs
For Monday's Mediterranean Bowl & Saturday's Comfort Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs boneless chicken thighs
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, rosemary, salt, and pepper
  2. Marinate chicken in mixture for at least 30 minutes (up to 4 hours)
  3. Preheat grill or grill pan to medium-high heat
  4. Grill chicken 6-8 minutes per side until internal temp reaches 165¬∞F
  5. Let rest 5 minutes, then slice or chop
  6. Makes enough for multiple bowls throughout the week
Soy-Ginger Baked Tofu
For Tuesday's Asian-Inspired Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 2 blocks (28 oz total) extra-firm tofu
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon honey or maple syrup
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Press tofu between paper towels for 15 minutes to remove moisture
  2. Cut into 3/4-inch cubes
  3. Whisk together soy sauce, vinegar, sesame oil, ginger, garlic, honey, and red pepper flakes
  4. Marinate tofu in mixture for 15-30 minutes
  5. Preheat oven to 400¬∞F
  6. Spread tofu on parchment-lined baking sheet
  7. Bake 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until golden and crispy
Seasoned Black Beans
For Wednesday's Southwest Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 cup vegetable broth or water
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup corn kernels (frozen or fresh)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat
  2. Saut√© onion until softened, about 5 minutes
  3. Add garlic, cumin, chili powder, and paprika; cook 1 minute
  4. Add black beans, corn, and broth
  5. Simmer 8-10 minutes until flavors meld
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste
  7. Can be served warm or at room temperature
Smoked Paprika Roasted Chickpeas
For Thursday's Fall Harvest Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400¬∞F
  2. Pat chickpeas completely dry with paper towels
  3. Toss with olive oil and all spices until evenly coated
  4. Spread on a rimmed baking sheet in single layer
  5. Roast 25-35 minutes, shaking pan halfway through
  6. Chickpeas should be golden and slightly crispy
  7. Best served warm but good at room temperature too
Herb-Baked Salmon
For Friday's Protein Power Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb salmon fillets
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Lemon slices for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425¬∞F
  2. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper
  3. Mix olive oil, lemon juice, dill, parsley, garlic, salt, and pepper
  4. Place salmon on prepared baking sheet
  5. Brush herb mixture over salmon fillets
  6. Top with lemon slices
  7. Bake 12-15 minutes until fish flakes easily with a fork
  8. Internal temperature should reach 145¬∞F
Perfect Hard-Boiled Eggs
For Friday's Protein Power Bowl & Sunday's Clean-Out Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 6-8 large eggs
  • Water for boiling
  • Ice water for cooling

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil
  2. Gently lower eggs into boiling water with a slotted spoon
  3. Cook for exactly 10 minutes for firm yolks
  4. Immediately transfer to ice water bath
  5. Let cool for 5 minutes
  6. Peel under cool running water
  7. Store peeled eggs in refrigerator up to 1 week

Versatility: These proteins can be repurposed in different bowls throughout the week for variety.

Tahini-Miso Dressing
For Tuesday's Asian-Inspired Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon miso paste (white or yellow)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon honey or maple syrup
  • 2-3 tablespoons warm water (to thin)

Instructions:

  1. Whisk tahini and miso paste until smooth
  2. Add vinegar, soy sauce, sesame oil, ginger, and honey
  3. Slowly whisk in warm water until desired consistency
  4. Store in refrigerator, stir before using
  5. Makes about 1/2 cup
Lime-Cumin Vinaigrette
For Wednesday's Southwest Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Combine all ingredients in a jar with tight lid
  2. Shake vigorously until emulsified
  3. Let sit 10 minutes for flavors to meld
  4. Makes about 1/2 cup
Apple Cider Vinegar-Dijon Dressing
For Thursday's Fall Harvest Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 shallot, minced (or 2 tablespoons red onion)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Whisk mustard and honey until smooth
  2. Slowly whisk in vinegar, then gradually add olive oil
  3. Stir in shallot, salt, and pepper
  4. Taste and adjust seasoning
  5. Makes about 1/2 cup
Lemon-Dill Yogurt Dressing
For Friday's Protein Power Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1-2 tablespoons water (if needed to thin)

Instructions:

  1. Whisk yogurt until smooth
  2. Add lemon juice, olive oil, dill, garlic, salt, and pepper
  3. Thin with water if needed
  4. Best used within 3-4 days
  5. Makes about 3/4 cup
Balsamic Reduction
For Saturday's Comfort Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (to finish)

Instructions:

  1. Simmer balsamic vinegar in small saucepan over medium heat
  2. Reduce by half (about 10-15 minutes)
  3. Stir in honey
  4. Let cool slightly, then whisk in olive oil
  5. Drizzle over bowl just before serving
  6. Makes about 1/4 cup

üí° Tips for Success

Portion Control: Plan for about 3/4 cup cooked grain, 4-6 oz protein, and 1-2 cups vegetables per bowl.


Flavor Balance: Each bowl should have something creamy (avocado, cheese, yogurt dressing), something crunchy (nuts, seeds, fresh vegetables), and something acidic (citrus, vinegar).


Storage: Assemble bowls without dressing, store components separately, and dress just before eating.


Customization: Keep extra toppings available so family members can customize their bowls to preference.


Temperature: Warm your grains and proteins, keep fresh elements cool for the best contrast and food safety.
